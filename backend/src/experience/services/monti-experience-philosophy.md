# Monti Experience Philosophy

Monti creates small, self-contained interactive experiences that help people
understand something by engaging with it.

A Monti experience should not feel like a web page that happens to have
buttons. It should feel like a compact interactive model of an idea: something
the learner can act on, observe, test, and gradually understand.

Monti is for any learner, from early childhood to university and adulthood.
The goal is not to make everything simplistic. The goal is to match the rigor,
notation, autonomy, and tone to the learner while keeping the experience
interactive, focused, and clear.

The aim is not to maximize content coverage. The aim is to produce a moment of
real comprehension.

A good Monti experience helps a learner say:

- "I get this now because I tried it."
- "I could see what changed when I made that choice."
- "It was simple, but it taught me something real."

## Principles

### 1. Learning Through Interaction

The primary way a learner understands should be by doing something, not by
reading explanations.

### 2. One Idea, One Strong Core Loop

Each experience should revolve around a clear concept and a coherent mechanic.
Depth beats breadth.

### 3. Feedback Is The Teaching Layer

The system's response should help the learner understand cause and effect, not
just tell them right or wrong.

### 4. Simplify Reality Without Distorting It

Monti experiences can abstract, compress, or dramatize a concept, but they
should remain conceptually faithful.

### 5. Match The Learner, Respect The Learner

Monti should adapt to the learner's maturity, prior knowledge, and desired
rigor. It should not infantilize older learners or flatten advanced material.

### 6. Clarity Before Cleverness

The learner should quickly understand what they can do, what matters, and what
they are trying to discover.

### 7. Delight In Service Of Understanding

Motion, surprise, playfulness, and visual polish are valuable when they sharpen
attention and reinforce the idea.

### 8. Finishable, Robust, And Focused

An experience should be small enough to complete in one sitting and reliable
enough to work without confusion or fragility.

### 9. Agency Without Drift

The learner should have meaningful freedom to explore, but the experience
should still guide them toward a clear insight or outcome.

## What Monti Is

- A place for interactive understanding
- A generator of compact learning artifacts
- A product that favors intuition, feedback, and discovery
- A system for turning rough prompts into experiences that feel crafted

## What Monti Is Not

- A slide deck with animation
- A content page with a quiz attached
- A generic game skin wrapped around facts
- A static explainer pretending to be interactive
- A sandbox with no learning arc
- A flashy interface with weak educational value

## Quality Bar

A strong Monti experience should be:

- Immediately understandable
- Meaningfully interactive within seconds
- Focused on one concept or skill
- Responsive and feedback-rich
- Visually intentional
- Short enough to finish
- Strong enough to remember
- Appropriately rigorous for the learner and topic

## Rubric

### 1. Interactive Depth

Does the learner meaningfully act on the system?

### 2. Conceptual Clarity

Is the learning goal specific, coherent, and easy to grasp?

### 3. Feedback Quality

Does the system's response help the learner understand why something happened?

### 4. Mechanic Cohesion

Do the interactions reinforce one core idea and one core loop?

### 5. Faithfulness

Does the simplification preserve the truth of the concept?

### 6. Learner Fit

Does the tone, rigor, notation, and autonomy match the learner without
becoming patronizing or unnecessarily dense?

### 7. Usability

Can the learner start, continue, and recover without friction?

### 8. Engagement

Does it feel alive, satisfying, and worth completing?

### 9. Polish

Does it feel intentional and crafted rather than merely functional?

### 10. Completion Arc

Does it have a clear beginning, progression, and payoff?

## Non-Negotiables

- The learner must be able to do something meaningful quickly.
- The interaction must carry the learning, not just decorate it.
- The experience must teach one thing clearly.
- The system's feedback must deepen understanding.
- The rigor must match the learner and topic.
- The final result must feel focused and complete.

## Anti-Patterns

- "Read this, then answer questions."
- "Click tabs to reveal facts."
- "Right/wrong without explanation."
- "Too much setup before meaningful interaction."
- "Multiple weak mechanics competing for attention."
- "Beautiful UI with no real learning loop."
- "Ambitious simulation that is confusing or brittle."
- "Advanced topics watered down until they lose their real structure."
- "Older learners spoken to like children."

## Working Standard

Monti should consistently generate experiences that are:

- More interactive than explanatory
- More focused than comprehensive
- More insightful than impressive
- More crafted than templated
