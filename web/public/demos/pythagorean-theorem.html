Proof by Rearrangement

Discover why a² + b² = c² by shifting the triangles to reveal the hidden squares.

c² 25
a² 9
b² 16
1

The Setup

Four identical triangles sit inside a square. The triangles have legs a=3, b=4, and hypotenuse c=5. The central empty area is exactly c².

2

The Rearrangement

Slide the triangles. Since the large square boundary and the triangles themselves do not change size, the total amount of empty area remains exactly the same.

3

The Revelation

The empty area has split perfectly into two squares: a² and b². Because the total empty area didn't change, they must be equal to our starting space!

a² + b² = c²

Calculation Proof

Original empty area (c²): 5² = 25
New empty areas (a² + b²): 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25
Therefore: 25 = 25
